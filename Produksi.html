PRODUCTION FLOW

Sorting, Cleaning & Grading

This is the initial step where green coffee beans are meticulously sorted to remove any defects or foreign materials. This ensures that only the highest quality beans move forward in the production process.

Silo Storage

After sorting, the beans are stored in silos to maintain their freshness. This controlled environment protects the beans from moisture and contaminants until they are ready for further processing.

Roasting

Roasting is a critical phase where green coffee beans are heated to high temperatures, transforming them into the brown beans we recognize. This process develops the beans' flavor and aroma, with the roast level (light, medium, or dark) significantly influencing the final taste profile.

Blending

In this stage, different coffee beans are blended to create a unique flavor profile. Blending allows for the combination of beans with varying characteristics to achieve a balanced and consistent flavor.

Grinding

The roasted beans are ground to the desired size depending on the brewing method. The grind size can range from coarse for methods like French press to fine for espresso, each affecting the brewing process and flavor extraction.

Extraction

During extraction, water is used to extract soluble compounds from the ground coffee, creating the coffee liquid. This process is crucial as it determines the strength and flavor of the coffee.

Filtration & Centrifugation

Post-extraction, this process involves filtering out the remaining solids and centrifuging to ensure a clear coffee liquid, free of any residual particles.

Clarification & Evaporation

This step involves removing impurities and reducing water content to concentrate the coffee flavor. It's essential in producing a rich and full-bodied coffee product.

Flash-Pasteurization & Cold Storage

In this step, the coffee undergoes rapid heating and cooling to kill any harmful bacteria while preserving its flavor. The coffee is then stored in cold storage to maintain freshness until it is packaged.

Spray Drying

In this final stage, the coffee is transformed into a dry powder through a spray drying process, which involves converting the liquid coffee into fine particles, creating instant coffee that can be reconstituted with water.
